Section 6: Business Model Timing

Three factors make the economics work in 2026 that didn’t work in 2022:

  1. Subscription infrastructure maturity: RevenueCat, Superwall, and App Store subscription tools cost $200–500/month in 2026 vs. $50K–$100K in custom engineering in 2022.
  2. Cross-platform cost collapse: Flutter reduced multi-platform build cost from $800K–$1.2M (native) to $350K engineering.
  3. Creator-to-product CAC economics: Social-to-App Store conversion path is 3–4 taps in 2026 vs. 7–10 taps in 2022. Lower friction = higher conversion = lower effective CAC.

The $350K engineering build at $35 CAC with 2.6:1 LTV:CAC is a 2026-specific opportunity.

Push notifications Firebase Cloud Messaging Parent notifications only — NOT sent to child accounts

COPPA-First Architecture Constraints (non-negotiable):

  1. No persistent device identifiers on child accounts (session tokens only)
  2. Parental email-verified consent before any child data collection
  3. Zero third-party ad SDKs — Apple Kids Category and COPPA both prohibit behavioral advertising
  4. Data minimization: collect only child age-range, progress data, subscription status
  5. Parental dashboard as first-class feature: view activity, delete data, manage subscription, set time limits
[Flutter App — iOS / Android / Web]
        │
        ├── Supabase Auth (parental consent gate, JWT sessions)
        ├── Supabase DB (progress, profiles, entitlements cache)
        ├── Sanity CMS (dinosaur content, curriculum metadata)
        ├── RevenueCat SDK (subscription state, entitlements)
        ├── Cloudflare CDN (media assets, offline bundle prefetch)
        └── Plausible (anonymous pageview / session analytics)

Offline Mode: On subscription activation, prefetch content bundle for 5 starter dinos. Progress syncs to Supabase on reconnect with optimistic local writes.

The business case deliberately chose $49.99/year to position below ABCmouse, maximize subscriber velocity, and build App Store review moat faster. This is a defensible strategic choice — but it materially changes the financial profile.

If $9.99/month pricing were used: - 3-year revenue: ~$2.97M - Operating breakeven at Month 9–11 (vs. Month 15) - Year 2 LTV:CAC improves from 1.7:1 to ~3.1:1

The pricing decision is the central financial trade-off. Lower price = faster subscribers and deeper moat; higher price = better unit economics and faster breakeven.
